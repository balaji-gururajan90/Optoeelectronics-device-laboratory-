Research Themes

Exploring the Frontiers of Thin-Film Optoelectronics

Theme 01

Thin-Film & Transparent Solar Cells

Developing next-generation photovoltaic devices with enhanced efficiency and transparency for building-integrated applications

Chalcogenide Solar Cells

RF magnetron sputtering of CZTS, BiS, and CuBiS absorber layers for earth-abundant, non-toxic solar cells. Focused optimization of material composition, crystallinity, and defect density for enhanced photovoltaic performance.

  • CZTS, BiS, CuBiS absorber optimization
  • Non-toxic material development
  • Sputtering process control
  • Defect engineering

Transparent Photovoltaics

Design and fabrication of semi-transparent and fully transparent solar cells for window applications, greenhouses, and building-integrated photovoltaics (BIPV). Focus on balancing power conversion efficiency with visible light transmission.

  • Transparent conductive oxides (TCO)
  • Ultra-thin absorber layers
  • Wavelength-selective absorption
  • Aesthetic color tuning

Buffer Layer Engineering

Investigation of alternative buffer layers including ZnOS, ZnO, and AZO to replace toxic CdS. Development of band alignment strategies to minimize interface recombination losses.

Device Architecture Optimization

Systematic study of layer configurations, interface engineering, and contact optimization to maximize open-circuit voltage, short-circuit current, and fill factor.

Theme 02

Oxide-Semiconductor Photodetectors

Advanced UV and X-ray detection using wide-bandgap oxide semiconductors

β-Ga₂O₃ Photodetectors

Optimization of beta-phase gallium oxide thin films for solar-blind UV detection. RF sputtering parameter studies including oxygen partial pressure, substrate temperature, and post-deposition annealing effects.

  • High responsivity (>10 A/W)
  • Fast response time (<1 ms)
  • Solar-blind detection (λ < 280 nm)
  • Low dark current

Delafossite UV Photodetectors

Exploration of delafossite materials (e.g., CuAlO₂) for efficient UV photodetection. Focus on wide-bandgap p-type oxides to create high-performance pn-heterojunction devices with excellent selectivity.

X-ray Photodetectors

Development of thin-film X-ray detectors for medical imaging and security applications using high-Z materials and optimized device structures for enhanced X-ray absorption and charge collection efficiency.

Material Characterization

Comprehensive optical, electrical, and structural characterization including UV-Vis spectroscopy, Hall effect measurements, XRD, SEM, and photoluminescence studies.

Device Performance Metrics

Systematic evaluation of responsivity, detectivity, quantum efficiency, response time, and spectral selectivity under various operating conditions.

Theme 03

2D Materials & Heterostructures

Exploring transition metal dichalcogenides for next-generation optoelectronics

WS₂–MoS₂ Heterostructures

Fabrication and characterization of type-II heterostructures combining tungsten disulfide and molybdenum disulfide for enhanced charge separation and photocurrent generation.

Optoelectronic Properties

Investigation of bandgap engineering, exciton dynamics, and quantum efficiency in 2D material-based photodetectors and photovoltaic devices.

Theme 04

Computational Device Modeling

Numerical simulation and optimization of optoelectronic devices

SCAPS-1D Simulation

Solar cell capacitance simulator for one-dimensional device modeling. Systematic analysis of layer thickness, doping concentration, defect density, and interface properties on device performance.

  • J-V characteristic prediction
  • Quantum efficiency calculations
  • Defect state analysis
  • Interface recombination studies

COMSOL Multiphysics

Finite element analysis for complex device geometries and multi-physics coupling. Optical, electrical, and thermal simulations for comprehensive device understanding.

  • 3D device modeling
  • Optical field distribution
  • Carrier transport simulation
  • Thermal management analysis

Novel Buffer Layer Screening

Computational screening of alternative buffer materials (Zn-based, Sb-based compounds) for CZTS and other chalcogenide solar cells to identify optimal band alignment and minimize interface recombination.

Performance Optimization

Parametric studies and sensitivity analysis to identify critical parameters and guide experimental efforts toward maximum efficiency and stability.
