About Optoelectronics Device Lab

Our Mission, Vision & Team

Our Mission

Driving Innovation in Thin-Film Technologies

Mission

Optoelectronics Device Lab is dedicated to advancing the frontiers of thin film solar cells, transparent solar cells, UV, X-ray photodetectors and other devices. Our interdisciplinary team of researchers and students collaborates to explore innovative materials, fabrication techniques, and device architectures aimed at enhancing the efficiency, durability, and cost-effectiveness of thin film and solar technologies.

Vision

To advance a deep, physics-driven understanding of functional thin-film materials and devices, translating fundamental discoveries into robust optoelectronic technologies for energy harvesting, sensing, and intelligent systems, while nurturing globally engaged researchers equipped to address emerging societal and technological challenges.

Research Ecosystem

Our research ecosystem integrates fundamental materials science, advanced fabrication techniques, computational modeling, and device characterization. We maintain strong collaborations with industry partners and international research institutions, ensuring our work has both academic impact and practical relevance.

Leadership

Principal Investigator

Dr. Balaji Gururajan

Assistant Professor

Department of Electrical Engineering, Yuan Ze University

With a PhD from Anna University, India (2015-2020), and a postdoctoral fellowship from Yuan Ze University (2021-2023, NSTC sponsored), Dr. Balaji Gururajan's expertise lies in fabricating IR photodetectors, thin-film solar cells, transparent solar cells, thin-film transistors, UV and X-ray photodetectors, and computational modelling of these devices. Dr. Gururajan is keen on forging collaborations with fellow researchers from various universities who are engaged in thin film studies and its diverse applications.
